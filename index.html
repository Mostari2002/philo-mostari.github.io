I. Presocratic philosophy

Presocratic philosophy refers to the schools of thought that preceded Socrates. During that time, some of these philosophers were concerned with the question from the nature of “how was the universe created?”, or “from what substance was it created?”

Ionian philosophers are usually referred to as the first Greek philosophers.

Thales, the first Ionian, proposed that the cosmos were created from a single element. He believed that water was the origin of all things because he thought that the earth is floating on water.

Anaximenes proposed that everything came from air, as he believed that the earth is floating in the air.

Heraclitus said that it is fire, as our bodies are as warm as fire, even though fire, in this context, can be interpreted as a metaphor for flux (change). This is because he believed that everything is constantly changing and that “change is the only constant”, or as he words it “You can never step into the same river twice”.

Empedocles, however, believed that the universe came into existence from the mixture or separation of the 4 natural elements: earth, water, fire, and air. He also thought that this process happens through the interaction of love and strife (hate) and that none of these powers dominates in this world.

Anaximander thought that the universe didn’t come from a specific element, but rather from an immaterial substance that can’t be described. He called that substance “Apeiron”

Democritus proposed that nature consists of indivisible components (small parts that can’t be cut into half anymore), which he called Atoms, and void.

Pythagoras gave philosophy its name “love of wisdom”. He had a mathematical approach to philosophy: everything in the universe adheres to mathematical rules, so if you understand numbers and mathematics, you’ll understand the cosmos.
He also believed in reincarnation and the immortality of the soul.

Sophists believe that truth & morality are relative because what is true for one person may not be true for another.
Sophists also reject superstition.
